Design-Build

One Team. One Contract. No Gaps.

The traditional approach to building splits your project into separate vendors - an architect who designs without knowing your budget, a contractor who builds without owning the design, and an owner in the middle managing the conflict. Overruns, miscommunication, and scope gaps are built into the model.

Design-build solves this at the root. When design and construction live under one roof, decisions get made faster, costs stay aligned from day one, and you deal with one point of accountability for everything. That's the Koze model - and it's why our clients don't experience the surprises that define traditional construction.

What's Included

Everything Under One Roof

Design-build isn't a single service - it's the full stack. Here's what we cover from first conversation through final handover.

01

Architectural Design

Space planning, massing, siting, and permit-ready architectural drawings - coordinated through our design partners and fully integrated with the build side of our team. You get one point of contact, not two separate firms with separate agendas.

02

Interior Design & Finishes

Material selections, millwork details, fixture specifications, and finish schedules - curated by our design team and costed in real time. What you choose is what you're quoted, with no budget surprises after selections are made.

03

Permits & Compliance

We manage all permit applications, municipal submissions, and consultant coordination required by the authority having jurisdiction. You don't chase inspectors or interpret code - we handle it, start to finish.

04

Construction Management

Scheduling, trade coordination, site supervision, and quality control - managed by a dedicated project manager on your file. Weekly reporting keeps you informed without requiring you to be on site.

05

Consultant Coordination

Structural engineers, mechanical designers, landscape architects, geotechnical consultants - we manage every specialist required for your project. No separate contracts for you to manage, no coordination falling through the cracks.

06

Budget Alignment

Our process keeps design and budget in lockstep. We don't present you with a beautiful design and then ask you to value-engineer it back into range. The fixed-price contract only comes after design is complete and costs are confirmed.

The Koze Difference

Design-Build vs. Traditional

The difference between design-build and the traditional approach isn't just convenience - it's an entirely different risk profile for you as a client.

Traditional Approach Koze Design-Build
Design Responsibility Architect designs; contractor builds. Disputes over design intent are common - and you're in the middle. One team owns design and construction. No handoff, no gaps, no finger-pointing.
Budget Alignment Architect designs without construction knowledge; cost overruns discovered late in the process. Costs tracked against budget at every design stage. Scope and price aligned before permits are filed.
Contract Structure Multiple separate contracts - architect, GC, consultants - each with their own scope, liability, and terms. One fixed-price contract covering design, permits, and construction. One relationship, full accountability.
Cost Surprises Frequent mid-project change orders as design intent meets construction reality. Costs escalate unpredictably. Fixed-price issued only after full design and permit-readiness. Surprises are eliminated, not managed.
Coordination Owner coordinates between architect, GC, and consultants - a full-time role with real professional risk. We coordinate all consultants and trades internally. Owners stay informed, not involved in management.
Accountability When problems arise, contractors and architects dispute responsibility. The resolution process is slow and costly. We own every outcome - design and construction. One team accountable for the whole result.
Is This Right For You?

Who Benefits from Design-Build

Custom Home Clients

You've got a vision, a lot, and a budget - and you want a team that can take you from blank page to keys without the coordination burden. Our design-build model is purpose-built for this: one contract, one team, and a clear process that keeps you informed without requiring you to manage it.

Whole-Home Renovation Clients

Full-home renovation projects are the most complex residential builds - existing structure, permit compliance, phased occupancy. When design and construction are separated, that complexity doubles. Our integrated model keeps scope, budget, and timeline locked from concept through completion.

Investment Property Developers

ADUs, duplexes, and small multiplexes have real ROI thresholds - and cost overruns kill returns. A fixed-price design-build contract means your project economics are locked in before construction begins. We design to your investment parameters, not around them.

Design-Build FAQ

In the traditional model, you hire an architect and a general contractor separately - two contracts, two sets of liability, often conflicting incentives. When budget overruns or scope gaps emerge, the client ends up in the middle trying to resolve disputes between teams. Design-build puts design and construction under one contract. There's a single point of accountability for every decision and outcome - which means problems get resolved faster and the client doesn't carry that coordination burden.

It starts with a qualification call to confirm the project is a good mutual fit. From there: discovery and goal-setting, preliminary design and feasibility, full design and pre-construction, and then a fixed-price contract once everything is defined. Construction follows. At every stage, you know where things stand - we use a client portal to keep you informed without requiring you to manage the day-to-day. The goal is a guided, turnkey experience from first conversation to final walkthrough.

Budget is tracked against your goals throughout design, decisions are made with the construction side at the table, and there's no gap between what gets designed and what gets built.
